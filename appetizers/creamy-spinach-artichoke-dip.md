# Creamy Spinach & Artichoke Dip

## Overview

A rich, creamy baked spinach and artichoke dip for parties, BBQs, holidays, and game days. This upgraded party-size version uses cream cheese, sour cream, mayonnaise, mozzarella, Parmesan, Gruyère, fresh spinach, marinated artichoke hearts, garlic, and a buttery Parmesan-panko topping.

Serve warm with sourdough bread, mini sweet peppers, tortilla chips, crackers, or toasted baguette slices.

## Yield

One large 9x13-inch pan.

This is a heavy party-size batch. It can be assembled ahead, refrigerated, and baked before serving.

## Ingredients

### Dip base

- 2 (8 oz) blocks cream cheese, softened
- 1 cup sour cream
- 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- 10 oz fresh baby spinach
- 24 oz marinated artichoke hearts, drained well and chopped
- 16 oz shredded mozzarella, divided
- 5 oz grated Parmesan, divided
- 6 oz Gruyère, shredded, divided
- 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp kosher salt, or to taste
- 1 tsp black pepper

### Buttery Parmesan-panko topping

- 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 3 tbsp melted butter
- Reserved Parmesan, about 1 oz
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- Fresh cracked black pepper

## Method

1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
2. Lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
3. Drain the marinated artichoke hearts very well and chop them.
4. Wilt the fresh spinach in a large skillet over medium heat, working in batches if needed.
5. Transfer wilted spinach to a colander.
6. When cool enough to handle, squeeze out as much liquid as possible.
7. Roughly chop the squeezed spinach.
8. In a large mixing bowl, combine softened cream cheese, sour cream, and mayonnaise until smooth.
9. Stir in minced garlic, salt, and black pepper.
10. Fold in most of the mozzarella, most of the Parmesan, and most of the Gruyère, reserving about 2 cups mozzarella, 1 oz Parmesan, and 1 cup Gruyère for the top/topping.
11. Fold in chopped spinach and chopped artichoke hearts.
12. Taste and adjust salt carefully. Marinated artichokes and Parmesan can already be salty.
13. Spread the dip mixture evenly into the baking dish.
14. Top with the reserved mozzarella and Gruyère.
15. In a small bowl, mix panko breadcrumbs, melted butter, reserved Parmesan, garlic powder, and cracked black pepper.
16. Sprinkle the panko topping evenly over the cheese layer.
17. Bake at 350°F for 35-40 minutes, until the edges are bubbling and the center is hot.
18. If the topping needs more color, broil for 1-2 minutes, watching closely.
19. Let rest about 10 minutes before serving.

## Make-ahead notes

- The dip can be assembled several hours ahead and refrigerated before baking.
- If baking straight from the refrigerator, add about 5-10 minutes to the bake time.
- Add the panko topping right before baking if possible so it stays crisp.

## Serving

Good dippers:

- Sliced sourdough bread
- Mini sweet peppers
- Tortilla chips
- Crackers
- Toasted baguette slices

## Notes

- Squeezing the spinach dry is important so the dip does not turn watery.
- Gruyère adds a nutty richness and works well with the mozzarella and Parmesan.
- Marinated artichoke hearts add extra flavor, but they must be drained well.
- Letting the dip rest after baking helps it thicken before serving.
- The buttery Parmesan-panko topping adds texture and makes this version better for parties than a plain melted-cheese top.
