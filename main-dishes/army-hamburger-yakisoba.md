# Army Hamburger Yakisoba

Scaled-down home version of the U.S. Army Armed Forces Recipe Service **Hamburger Yakisoba (Ground Beef)** recipe, reduced from the 100-portion field kitchen version to a small batch using **1 lb ground beef**.

## What was cooked

A DFAC-style hamburger yakisoba made with ground beef, spaghetti noodles, green pepper, onion, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, black pepper, and green onions.

## Equipment used

- Large skillet or Dutch oven
- Medium pot for spaghetti
- Colander
- Measuring cups and spoons
- Cutting board and knife

## Yield

About **3 to 4 servings**.

The original Army recipe yields 100 portions at 1 cup each and uses 30 lb of raw 90% lean ground beef. This version uses a 1:30 scale factor, then rounds to normal kitchen measurements.

## Ingredients

- 1 lb 90% lean ground beef
- 3 to 4 oz dry spaghetti noodles
- Water for boiling spaghetti
- 1/4 tsp salt for pasta water
- 1/2 cup green bell pepper, julienned or thin sliced
- 1/2 cup yellow onion, chopped
- 1 tbsp soy sauce, plus more to taste
- 1/2 tsp salt, or to taste
- 1/4 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp ground ginger
- Pinch black pepper
- 1/4 cup water
- 2 tbsp green onions, sliced

## Process

1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until tender, about 10 to 12 minutes. Drain and set aside.
2. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, cook the ground beef over medium heat, breaking it apart as it browns. Drain excess fat if needed.
3. Add the chopped onion, green bell pepper, soy sauce, salt, garlic powder, ground ginger, and black pepper. Cook until the onion is tender, about 8 to 10 minutes.
4. Add 1/4 cup water, the sliced green onions, and the cooked spaghetti. Toss everything together until the noodles are coated and the pan is hot throughout.
5. Taste and adjust with a little more soy sauce, salt, pepper, or ginger if needed.

## Timing

- Prep: 10 minutes
- Cook: 20 minutes
- Total: about 30 minutes

## What worked

- Scaling from the beef amount keeps the recipe close to the Army version instead of turning it into a generic yakisoba.
- The official recipe is meat-heavy compared with restaurant-style yakisoba. That is part of the DFAC feel.
- Using spaghetti keeps it true to the Army recipe.

## What to change next time

- For a saucier version, increase soy sauce to 1 1/2 to 2 tbsp.
- For more noodle-heavy family portions, increase spaghetti to 6 to 8 oz while keeping the beef and vegetables the same.
- For a little more flavor without drifting too far, add a splash of Worcestershire sauce or a small pinch of sugar.

## Source note

Adapted from U.S. Army / Armed Forces Recipe Service recipe **L 062 01, Hamburger Yakisoba (Ground Beef)**. Original recipe: 100 portions, 1 cup each, with 30 lb raw 90% lean ground beef.
