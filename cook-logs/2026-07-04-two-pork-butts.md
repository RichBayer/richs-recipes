- If not near 190°F by noon, raise smoker to 275°F while wrapped.
- If seriously behind after 1:00 PM, raise smoker to 300°F while wrapped.

## Results

- Texture: probe tender before both butts reached the planned 203°F target; bone pulled clean after rest.
- Flavor: pork smelled and tasted excellent.
- Juices: butcher paper collected golden pork juices; save and mix some back into pulled pork as needed.
- Sauce level: sauces served on the side so pork stayed flexible.
- Family feedback: successful cook; worth using as the baseline pork butt process.
- Abi feedback: pork packed separately for travel.

## Lessons from this cook

- A late start can be recovered if the bark is built first and the smoker temperature is increased after wrapping.
- Wrap based on bark being set and dry, not temperature alone.
- Once wrapped, increasing the smoker to 275°F helped push through the stall without hurting bark quality.
- Finish by probe tenderness, not by chasing a specific internal temperature.
- If the pork is probe tender at 198-201°F, it is done.
- Resting wrapped in a towel-lined cooler is worth protecting.
- Save the pork juices from the butcher paper and add them back lightly when pulling.

## Change next time

- Consider leaving extra salt out of the injection if using Famous Dave's Steak & Burger seasoning again.
- If the meal has a fixed deadline, confirm the alarm is actually enabled the night before.
- For a 5:00 PM meal with two 9 lb butts, the original 3:00 AM meat-on target remains safer, but a 6:15 AM start can work with a 275°F wrapped finish.
