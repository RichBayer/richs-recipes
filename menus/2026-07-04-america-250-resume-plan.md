## Meat checkpoints

### Start

- Smoker at 250°F.
- Water pan installed.
- Probes routed cleanly.
- One probe in each butt.
- Butts placed with airflow around each one.

### Spritz

Only spritz if the surface looks dry after bark has started forming.

Spritz mix:

- 1/2 apple juice
- 1/2 apple cider vinegar

Do not spritz early and do not open the smoker constantly.

### Wrap

Wrap in butcher paper when both are true:

- Internal temp is around 160-170°F.
- Bark is set and does not wipe off easily.

Add a light spritz before closing the paper if desired. Do not soak.

### Finish

- Start checking tenderness around 198°F.
- Target is usually 202-205°F.
- Final decision is probe tenderness, not the number alone.
- Probe should slide in with very little resistance.

### Rest

- Keep wrapped in butcher paper.
- Wrap in foil if leaking.
- Wrap in towels.
- Hold in dry cooler.
- Preferred rest: 2-4 hours.
