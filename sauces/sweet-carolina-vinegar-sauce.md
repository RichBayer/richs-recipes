# Mild Carolina Vinegar Sauce

## Style

A mild Carolina-style vinegar sauce for pulled pork. This leans toward a Lexington/Piedmont-style dip: tangy apple cider vinegar, a little ketchup for roundness and color, brown sugar for balance, and savory seasoning.

Not mustard-based. Not spicy.

## Ingredients

- 2 cups apple cider vinegar
- 1/2 cup ketchup
- 1/3 cup brown sugar
- 1 tbsp white sugar, optional for extra balance
- 2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, optional
- 1/4 cup apple juice, optional for a softer family-style finish

## Method

1. Add all ingredients to a small saucepan.
2. Warm over medium-low heat for 5-10 minutes, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
3. Do not boil hard.
4. Cool before bottling.
5. Refrigerate until serving.
6. Shake before using.

## Use

Serve on the side with pulled pork so people can choose their own sauce level.

Use lightly when mixing into pulled pork. The pork should stay pork-forward, not drowned in sauce.

## Adjustments

- Sweeter: add a little more brown sugar.
- Softer/less sharp: add apple juice.
- Tangier: add more apple cider vinegar.
- More savory: add a pinch more salt, garlic powder, or onion powder.
- Spicy version for Rich: add hot sauce or red pepper flakes to a separate small bottle, not the main family batch.

## Notes

Classic Carolina vinegar sauces are thin and tangy. Lexington/Piedmont-style sauces usually add a little tomato or ketchup and sugar for balance, which works well for pulled pork sandwiches and family BBQ.
