# Smoked Pork Butt - Sweet Carolina Vinegar Style

## Overview

A slightly sweet pulled pork with Carolina-style vinegar bite. No mustard binder. Designed for a Pit Boss Brunswick vertical smoker, but adaptable to any pellet smoker.

## Equipment

- Pit Boss Brunswick vertical smoker
- Meat probes
- Heavy-duty foil or butcher paper
- Foil pans
- Cooler and towels for resting
- Meat claws or gloved hands for pulling

## Ingredients

- 1 pork butt, usually 7-10 lb
- No-mustard binder
- House pork rub
- Sweet Carolina vinegar sauce
- Apple juice or apple cider vinegar for wrapping

## No-mustard binder

- 1/2 cup apple juice
- 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
- 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tbsp hot sauce, optional

Use just enough binder to lightly wet the outside of the pork so the rub sticks.

## Prep

1. Pat pork butt dry.
2. Trim loose fat, hard chunks, and any ragged edges.
3. Lightly coat with no-mustard binder.
4. Apply pork rub generously on all sides.
5. Rest in the refrigerator overnight if possible.

## Smoke plan

1. Preheat smoker to 225°F.
2. Fill the water pan.
3. Place pork butt on the rack with room for airflow.
4. Smoke until internal temperature reaches about 160-170°F and the bark is set.
5. Wrap tightly in foil or butcher paper with a small splash of apple juice or vinegar.
6. Continue cooking until internal temperature reaches about 200-205°F.
7. Confirm doneness by probe feel. The probe should slide in with little resistance.
8. Rest wrapped for at least 1 hour, preferably 2-4 hours.
9. Pull pork and mix with a small amount of reserved juices and sweet Carolina vinegar sauce.

## Serving

Serve on soft buns with slaw, pickles, and extra vinegar sauce on the side.

## Notes

- Done early is better than done late. Pork butt holds well wrapped in a cooler.
- For a sweeter family-style finish, sauce lightly after pulling and serve more sauce at the table.
- For travel, pack pulled pork in a covered foil pan with sauce separate.
